==Solution 1==
Rearranging, we find $3x+y=-2x+6y$, or $5x=5y\implies x=y$.
Substituting, we can convert the second equation into $\frac{x+3x}{3x-x}=\frac{4x}{2x}=\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$.



More step-by-step explanation:

$\frac{3x+y}{x-3y}=-2$

$3x+y=-2\left(x-3y\right)$

$3x+y=-2x+6y$

$5x=5y$

$x=y$

$\frac{x+3y}{3x-y}=\frac{1+3\left(1\right)}{3\left(1\right)-1}=\frac{4}{2}=2$.

We choose $\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 2}$.
