==Problem==
Which of the following conditions is sufficient to guarantee that integers $x$, $y$, and $z$ satisfy the equation
$$x(x-y)+y(y-z)+z(z-x) = 1?$$

$\textbf{(A)} \: x>y$ and $y=z$

$\textbf{(B)} \: x=y-1$ and $y=z-1$

$\textbf{(C)} \: x=z+1$ and $y=x+1$

$\textbf{(D)} \: x=z$ and $y-1=x$

$\textbf{(E)} \: x+y+z=1$
