==Solution 1==

We can rewrite $10^{x}\cdot 100^{2x}=1000^{5}$ as $10^{5x}=10^{15}$:
$$\begin{split}
10^x\cdot100^{2x} & =10^x\cdot(10^2)^{2x} \\
10^x\cdot10^{4x} & =(10^3)^5 \\
10^{5x} & =10^{15}
\end{split}$$
Since the bases are equal, we can set the exponents equal, giving us $5x=15$. Solving the equation gives us $x = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\;3}.$
